The Artificial Lungs of the Earth

The Artificial Lungs of the Planet project has overarching goals to address a range of environmental and accessibility issues.

The project is aimed at reducing the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere, stabilizing the global temperature on Earth, improving the climate in arid regions, optimizing the use of areas for the development of the economy and the extraction of minerals from sea water.

  • Decrease in air temperature due to surface evaporation.
  • Increase in air humidity.
  • Mining from sea water where the salt will naturally accumulate.
  • Ability to grow various plants, including crops.
  • Reducing the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.
  • Reducing the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.
  • The possibility of using this technology in construction, including the construction of walls and fences, as well as in the planning of buildings and structures.
  • Possibility of creating vertical fields with artificial irrigation.

This project is a unique solution that allows the use of vertical space for growing plants using sea water. This can have a positive contribution to solving environmental problems and ensuring sustainable development of the planet. However, the successful implementation of the project requires detailed research, testing and cooperation with experts and organizations in the field of ecology and agricultural development. It is also necessary to consider the payback of the project and its long-term sustainability.
